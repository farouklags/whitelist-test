About

A patient eye for empty places.

Mara Voss makes black-and-white landscape photographs in the quiet margins of the day — the slow hour before light arrives and the long grey after it leaves. Working from Reykjavík and travelling widely, she returns to coastlines, lava fields and weather-worn interiors, waiting for the moment a place falls still.

Her work strips a scene to tone, line and texture, trusting silence over spectacle. Across editorial commissions, limited fine-art editions and field workshops, the aim stays the same: photographs that hold their breath, and ask you to slow down with them.

  • Based in Reykjavík — working worldwide
  • Selected clients: Norðan Review, Atlas Quarterly, Fjord & Field, Hólar Editions
  • Represented prints held in private collections across Europe

What I Offer

  • Editorial commissions

    Assignments for journals and brands that value stillness — long, unhurried days spent waiting for the right grey light.

  • Fine-art prints

    Hand-finished archival editions on cotton rag, printed in small numbers and signed for collectors and quiet walls.

  • Workshops & talks

    Small-group field sessions and gallery talks on seeing in monochrome, patience, and the craft of the landscape frame.
